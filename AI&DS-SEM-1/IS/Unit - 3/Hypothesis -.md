Hypothesis testing is ==a formal statistical procedure that uses **sample data to evaluate two mutually exclusive statements (hypotheses) about a population** to determine which statement the evidence best supports==. It provides a data-driven framework for making reliable conclusions and separating genuine effects from those that could have occurred by random chance. 

- You start with two competing statements:
    - Null hypothesis (H0): the default claim (e.g., μ = μ0, p = p0).
    - Alternative hypothesis (H1 or Ha): what you want evidence for (e.g., μ ≠ μ0, μ > μ0, p < p0).
