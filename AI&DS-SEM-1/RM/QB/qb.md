A university research team is studying whether using interactive digital learning tools improves students’ academic performance compared to traditional lecture-based teaching.
They select 60 undergraduate students and randomly divide them into two groups:
•	Group A (30 students): Taught using interactive digital tools.
•	Group B (30 students): Taught using traditional lecture methods.
After one semester, both groups take the same standardized test.
•	Group A’s average score = 78 (standard deviation = 8)
•	Group B’s average score = 72 (standard deviation = 10)
At a 5% level of significance, the research team wants to determine whether interactive digital learning tools significantly improve performance compared to traditional teaching.
Tasks:
1.	Formulate the null hypothesis (H₀) and alternative hypothesis (H₁).
2.	Identify whether it is a one-tailed or two-tailed test, and justify.
3.	Choose the appropriate statistical test for this scenario.
4.	Carry out the test step by step (up to the test statistic calculation, critical value identification, and decision-making).
5.	Write a conclusion in simple language for the university research team.
